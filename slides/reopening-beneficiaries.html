Executive Summary
The Strait of Hormuz reopening creates a clear tiered benefit structure across industries. Tier 1 beneficiaries (airlines, shipping, retail) see immediate margin expansion from falling fuel costs and normalized shipping routes—Delta and United expecting record free cash flow. Tier 2 sectors (materials, industrials, autos) benefit as supply chains normalize over 1-3 months, though helium constraints persist for semiconductors. Tier 3 industries (agriculture, pharma) face structural lags of 6-9 months due to long-term contracts and API supply chains. Key risk: deal implementation—full normalization depends on June 19 signing and sustained compliance. Energy and defense face headwinds as war premiums unwind.
